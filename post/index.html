June 1, 2021

PCA for Anomaly Detection

Collaborators: Tim Wickard Description: This project was done as consulting work for a manufacturing plant which had a vast amount of process data from their plant’s sensor network (data have been anonymized here). The goal was to build a model to detect anomalies in the data that could contribute to defects in the produced membranes. We used Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to find anomalous data and learn which features in the sensor network contributed the most to this anomalous behavior.

Association and other forms of positive dependence for Feller evolution systems

(Paper published in “Theory of Probability and Its Applications” in January 2021) Abstract: We characterize various forms of positive dependence for a general class of time-inhomogeneous Markov processes called Feller evolution processes (FEPs) and for jump-FEPs. General FEPs can be studied through their time and state-space dependent (extended) generators. We will use the time and state-space dependent (extended) generators and time and state-space dependent LÃ©vy measures to characterize the positive dependence of general FEPs and jump-FEPs, respectively.
